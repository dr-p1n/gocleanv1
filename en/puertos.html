Ports & Logistics Zones

Operations that never stop demand cleaning that never fails.

A port operates under constant regulatory pressure: maritime-authority inspections, shipping-line audits, and international free-zone sanitation standards. A cleaning failure isn't cosmetic — it can lead to held cargo, fines, or lost contracts with international operators. Cleaning port terminals requires protocols that coexist with heavy machinery, continuous shifts, and regulated substances.

What we cover

  • Cleaning of terminals, cargo warehouses, and transit zones
  • Handling of industrial waste and controlled substances
  • Protocols compatible with 24/7 operations without disrupting logistics flow
  • Compliance with free-zone and maritime-authority sanitary regulations

Backed by ISO 9001 certification, ISSA and APAFAM membership, with operational experience in high-complexity logistics zones and national coverage across 9 provinces.
